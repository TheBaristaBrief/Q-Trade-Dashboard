Strait of Hormuz Tensions Escalate — Qatar's Entire LNG Export Route Under Threat
Heightened military posturing between Iran and US naval forces in the Persian Gulf has raised acute concerns about the viability of the Strait of Hormuz as a trade corridor. The strait is the world's most critical energy chokepoint — approximately 97% of Qatar's LNG exports and 95% of all Qatar seaborne trade transit through it. Any disruption, even temporary, would halt Qatar's primary revenue stream. QatarEnergy's emergency diversion protocols are under active review. The Sultanate of Oman's Port of Duqm is being evaluated as a partial contingency routing option via overland pipeline.

UN Comtrade — Goods Trade Database

The world's most comprehensive merchandise trade statistics database. Qatar data available at HS2 (chapter) → HS4 (heading) → HS6 (subheading, maximum available). Also captures transport mode codes (motCode: 1=Sea, 2=Rail, 3=Road, 4=Air, 5=Postal, 9=Other) — the basis for Tab 3's logistics analysis.

Data.gov.qa — Qatar Open Data Portal

Qatar's national open data portal maintained by the Planning and Statistics Authority (PSA). Provides domestic trade statistics, Hamad Port throughput (TEU, bulk tonnage), free zone performance, and QFC data. Essential for ground-truth validation and port logistics analysis.

WTO BaTIS — Balanced Trade in Services

The WTO's Balanced Trade in Services dataset covering Mode 1–4 services trade under GATS framework using EBOPS 2010 classification. BaTIS reconciles asymmetries between reporting countries to provide the most reliable bilateral services trade data available for Qatar.

Geopolitical Shock Impact Assessment
Estimated Supply Disruption Risk per Commodity — Hormuz Closure Scenario
Hormuz Closure Scenario: A 30-day closure would disrupt ~97% of essential sea-borne imports. Items with GCC land routes (Jordan/Saudi Arabia road corridors) or significant local production have partial resilience. Strategic stockpile reserves held by MOCI estimated at 3–6 months for staples (rice, flour, sugar, cooking oil). Perishables (vegetables, fresh fish, chilled meat) have less than 2-week buffer.
